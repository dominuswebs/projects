Question [NOUN] は　[when/who/what]　が　いいですか

To try something

[NOUN] を　[VERB てform] みます I'm going to try [VERB] the [NOUN]

EX: この　ケーキ　を　た(食)べてみます I'm going to try(eating) the cake

 

Comparisons

[A]は[B]より[...]です/[...]ます[A] is more [...] than [B]

EX1: オーストラリア　は　にほん(日本) より　おお(大)きいです Australia is bigger than Japan

EX2: おおさか　は　おきなわ　より　にんき(人気)　が　ありまし Osaka is more popular than okinawa

Preference between 2 things

[A]　と　[B]　と　どちら　(が[ADJ]/を[VERB]) [XXX] です[ADJ]/ます[VERB] か. Which do you [XXX] better. [A] or [B]?

EX1 Q: ビールとワインと　どちらが　すきですか Which do you like better, beer or wine?

EX1 A: ビール　のほうが　(ワインより)　好きです。 I like beer better (than wine) - The text between paranthesis is optional

EX2 Q: Youtubeとテレビと　どちらを　よく　み(見)ますか。 Which do you watch more often, Youtube or TV?

EX2 A: youtube のほうが (テレビより)　よく　見ます。 I watch Youtube more often (than TV) - The text between paranthesis is optional

 

Expressing a sequence of events

Before...

まえ(前)にmeans "before". The verb coming before まえ(前)に is always in the dictionary form regardless of the tense of the verb at the end of the sentence.

EX1: 日本に　くる　前に、日本ご(語)の　べんきょうを　はじめました。I began my studies of Japanese before I came to Japan.

However if the emphasis in on the duration of the previous action, you often omit に.

EX2: 日本に　くる　前、２年[ねん]間[かん]　日本語[ご]の　べんきょうを　していました。 I had studied Japanese for 2 years before I came to Japan.

Expressing/Asking opinion

と　おもいます　This is the phrase to express our opinion or to ask someone theirs.

Expressing our opinion

あたらしい　プロジェクトには　お金[かね]が　たくさん　かかりますか Does a new project cost a lot of money?

The answer is in plain form.

YES: かかると　おもいます I think it will take.

NO: かからないと　おもいます I don't think it will take.

A社の　コンピュータより　安[やす]くて　いい　コンピュータが　ありますか Is there a better computer that is cheaper than A(company) computer?

YES: あると　おもいます

NO: あらないと　おもいます

Connecting related sentences

Noun で、Sentence です/ます

ミルズさんは　カナダ人で、かとうさんは　日本人です Mr. Mills is Canadian and Mr Katou is Japanese.

 

Nominalising sentences

plain form + の　transforms a sentence into a noun that becomes the topic for the rest of the sentence.

  • ...のは...です
  • ...のが...です
  • ...のを...ます - Usually only used with 3 verbs (わすれます、しっていますか、まっています)

仕事[しごと]が　おわるのは　７じごろです As for when the work ends, it is around 7

漢字[かんじ]をかくのはむずかしいです As for writing Kanji, it is difficult.
